Tham Phou Kham

Golden Crab Cave drive 100 meters into the ground after passing through the small entrance hole. However, the cave widens once inside, when where a reclining Buddha statue welcomes visitors to a place once serving as home to bats, gibbons, monkeys, and antelopes. Tham Pou Kham displays several wet rock formations including a pair of “Golden Crabs”. Don’t forget your torch. After discover cavern, go for a cold swim in the Ka River’s water hold. The water is clean, deep, and safe for jumping in from the trees, and a small shop and restaurant run by local serve meals and snacks. Location: Cross the Nam Xong in southern end of town at the Paradise Island Bridge to Ban Houay Nyae. Follow the dirt road past the village and turn right (north) at the sharp left-hand turn at Nathong Tai Village. The trail to the cave starts about 1 kilometer past the junction in Ban Nathong Neua.

Tham Chang Cave

Considered by many as Vang Vieng’s most important cave, Tham Chang (or Jung) became the home for migrating people, who settled near the town’s southern Meuang Xong Village to rise vegetables. The locals stumbled into the cavern during the civil war, while seeking refuge deep in the forest. Because its high location offered perfect view of Vang Vieng, the entire village moved there to sit out the war, and named their haven, Tham Jung (Hang Around Cave). Years later during the colonial era, the local returned to cultivating the nearby fields and would bathe in the cave’s basin, where the water was so cold they said it could freeze your legs to a firm stiffness. Then they adjusted the cave’s name to Tham Jung, which mean “unable to move”, leaving locals no choice but to “hang around”. Follow the river road south of Meuang Xong village, pay the entrance fee at the gate of Vang Vieng Resort and cross the bridge over the Nam Xong to the steps leading up the cliff to Tham Chang.

Kaeng Nyui Waterfall

Kaeng Nyui is a 30 meter waterfall situated on the eastern side of Vang Vieng town. Reaching the waterfall involves a walk along the Houay Nyui and Nam Lao streams past a number of cascades and a smaller waterfall called Kenlon falls. Kenlon has a drop of about 5 meters, into a lovely swimming pool about nine meters in diameter. Villagers from Ban Naduang are the traditional owners of the site, and have constructed the paths following the stream to both waterfalls, small bridges and other facilities, as well as maintaining swimming holes, and a picnic area along the stream and in the surrounding nature reserve. They also manage the car park and own the foodstalls. Pick up some local delicacies here for a picnic alongside the stream. Village Guides: If you would like a village guide, you can enquire in Naduang Village. A village guide can show and explain the nature, as well as how they traditionally use some of the forest products encountered on the trails.

Hot Air Ballooning

Hot air ballooning in Vang Vieng, Vientiane Province, is one of the most popular tourist activities in Laos. Vang Vieng is a small town located along the Nam Song River, surrounded by towering limestone mountains and breathtaking natural landscapes, making it an ideal destination for hot air balloon rides that offer panoramic aerial views. The popularity of hot air ballooning in Vang Vieng began around 2015-2016 when local operators introduced the activity, inspired by neighboring Myanmar, where balloon rides over Bagan had already gained international fame. With Vang Vieng’s stunning scenery, including the Nam Song River, rice fields, and dramatic karst mountains, hot air ballooning quickly became a major attraction, drawing visitors from around the world.

Initially, balloon services in Vang Vieng were limited to just a few flights per day, but as demand grew, operators expanded their services, ensuring that the balloons met international safety standards. Professional pilots, trained abroad, were brought in to manage the flights. Hot air balloon rides in Vang Vieng typically take place early in the morning or during the evening when the air is calmest. Passengers get the chance to witness breathtaking sunrises or sunsets from above, as the balloon soars over the Nam Song River and the surrounding limestone valleys. Each ride lasts approximately 30-40 minutes, reaching altitudes of around 500-800 meters.

The cost of a hot air balloon ride in Vang Vieng ranges from $90 to $120 per person, depending on the season and the service provider. This is relatively affordable compared to other countries like Myanmar or Turkey, where balloon rides can exceed $300 per flight. In terms of safety, the balloons used in Vang Vieng are regularly inspected and maintained by experts, and emergency response training is conducted. Passengers are also given safety instructions before the flight.

Hot air ballooning has significantly boosted Vang Vieng’s local economy, attracting international travelers seeking unique experiences beyond other popular activities such as kayaking, tubing on the Nam Song River, and exploring caves. The growth of the ballooning industry has also led to improvements in tourism infrastructure, including hotels, restaurants, and transportation services.

Overall, hot air ballooning in Vang Vieng is one of Laos' most distinctive tourist attractions, offering a unique perspective of the region’s stunning landscapes at an affordable price. With a strong focus on safety and well-managed operations, Vang Vieng continues to be a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Angnamtong

Namtong Reservoir is a water reservoir located in Muang Feuang, Vientiane Province, Laos. Originally constructed as a water source for agriculture and local communities, it has since become a popular destination for relaxation and tourism, especially in recent years. Surrounded by mountains and lush forests, Namtong Reservoir offers a peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of Vientiane's capital city.

The reservoir features crystal-clear water and stunning landscapes throughout the year. During the rainy season, the water level rises, and the surrounding nature becomes vibrant and green. In the dry season, although the water level may decrease slightly, the area remains beautiful, and various water activities can still be enjoyed. Because of this, Namtong Reservoir has become a favored spot for both tourists and locals seeking leisure and outdoor recreation.

Over the past few years, the reservoir’s popularity has steadily increased, leading to its development as an eco-tourism site. Floating huts are available for rent, allowing visitors to relax in the middle of the water. Paddle boats and pedal boats are also provided for those who wish to explore the reservoir at a leisurely pace. Additionally, small local restaurants offer traditional Lao cuisine, enhancing the experience for visitors looking to enjoy both nature and authentic flavors.

Namtong Reservoir is located about 90 minutes from Vientiane, making it a convenient getaway for a day trip. Travelers can reach the site by car or motorbike, and while it may not be internationally famous, it remains a must-visit for those who appreciate serene natural landscapes. The cost of visiting Namtong Reservoir is relatively affordable. Renting a floating hut costs around 50,000 LAK per day for a standard hut and 80,000 LAK per day for a hut with a built-in pool. The usage time is usually set until 5:30 PM, and additional fees may apply for extended hours.

Although little information is available regarding the reservoir's original construction, it is believed to have been built primarily for agricultural and household water supply. Over time, its stunning natural surroundings and tranquil environment led to its transformation into a tourism destination. Namtong Reservoir is a prime example of how water resources can serve both economic and recreational purposes.

Today, the reservoir continues to grow in popularity, especially on weekends and public holidays, when visitors from Vientiane and nearby provinces come to relax and unwind. Tourists often appreciate its peaceful atmosphere and breathtaking scenery, making it a perfect spot for families and friends to enjoy a refreshing getaway amidst nature.
