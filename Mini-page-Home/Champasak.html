Wat Phou

Wat Phou is an ancient temple complex located in Champasak Province, Laos, about 38 kilometers from Pakse. It is a Hindu-Brahmanic temple built during the 12th century and is believed to have been dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is situated at the base of Mount Lingaparvata, known locally as Phu Kao.
In 2001, UNESCO designated Wat Phou as Laos’ second World Heritage Site due to its historical and cultural significance. The temple complex was designed based on Hindu-Brahmanic cosmology, with Mount Phu Kao symbolizing Mount Meru, the mythical home of the gods.

Architectural Features

Wat Phou consists of several structures built in a linear layout ascending towards the mountain. The outermost part features three large reservoirs (baray), of which only two remain today. Beyond the reservoirs are two main buildings, known as the North and South Palaces or the “Men’s and Women’s Halls.” These U-shaped structures have entrances at the back and two exits in the front.
The central sanctuary, adorned with intricate carvings, was originally a Hindu shrine but was later converted into a Buddhist temple. Inside, a Buddha image was installed for worship when the site functioned as a Buddhist monastery.

Annual Festival and Cultural Significance

Wat Phou remains a sacred site for the Lao people. Every year, on the 3rd day of the 3rd lunar month, a grand religious festival is held at the temple. This festival reflects the deep spiritual and cultural connection that the people of Champasak and Laos have with Wat Phou.

Preservation Efforts

In 2022, Laos launched a conservation initiative by planting 1,565 frangipani trees around Wat Phou to commemorate its heritage. The frangipani tree is Laos' national flower, and the first known frangipani tree in the country was discovered at Phu Kao before being propagated across Laos.
